Writing Instructions for Literature Review
Target Profile

Authors: Undergraduate/graduate students in Computer Science
Audience: Academic reviewers, capstone committee
Tone: Professional but accessible, demonstrating understanding without overclaiming
Length: 6-8 pages (standard conference paper format)

Critical Writing Guidelines
PROMPT FOR LITERATURE REVIEW GENERATION:

You are writing a literature review section for a college capstone project on 
"Brand-Aware Content Generation Using GraphRAG and Web Scraping."

STRICT REQUIREMENTS:

1. TONE & VOICE:
   - Write as a senior undergraduate or early graduate student would
   - Show enthusiasm for the topic but acknowledge limitations
   - Use phrases like:
     * "Our investigation reveals..."
     * "We observe that..."
     * "Building on prior work..."
     * "This approach addresses..."
   - AVOID:
     * "Groundbreaking" / "Revolutionary" / "Novel" (overused AI words)
     * "Delve into" / "Dive deep" / "Harness" (AI clich廥)
     * "It is worth noting that" / "Moreover" / "Furthermore" (repetitive transitions)
     * Excessive use of "significant" / "crucial" / "essential"

2. STRUCTURE:
   Follow this organization:
   
   Section 2: Related Work
   2.1 Knowledge Graph Construction (AutoSchemaKG, GraphRAG Survey)
   2.2 Web Scraping and Brand Data Extraction (Logo detection, Color extraction)
   2.3 Image Quality Assessment (BRISQUE, Deep learning IQA)
   2.4 Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG optimization, Hybrid approaches)
   2.5 Brand Consistency in Generated Content (Brand voice, Style transfer)
   2.6 User Feedback and Preference Learning (DPO, Efficient RLHF)

3. WRITING STYLE:
   - Vary sentence structure (mix short and long sentences)
   - Use active voice when discussing methods: "We implement..." not "It was implemented..."
   - Include specific metrics when available: "achieved 77.6% MRR improvement"
   - Acknowledge gaps: "While X addresses Y, it does not handle Z, which our system tackles..."
   - Connect to your project: "This finding informs our design decision to..."

4. AVOID AI DETECTION:
   - NO lists within paragraphs (convert to narrative flow)
   - Vary vocabulary (use synonyms, don't repeat "approach" 15 times)
   - Include occasional contractions where appropriate: "doesn't", "we'll"
   - Use domain-specific jargon naturally: "Cypher queries", "Laplacian variance", "SDXL"
   - Add subtle imperfections: "roughly 85%", "approximately", "around"
   
5. ACADEMIC INTEGRITY:
   - Cite every claim with [AuthorYear] format
   - Paraphrase, don't quote extensively
   - Compare and contrast approaches
   - Identify gaps that justify your project

6. SPECIFIC VOCABULARY TO AVOID (AI red flags):
   ? "Delve"
   ? "Intricate"
   ? "Robust" (use "reliable", "stable", "effective" instead)
   ? "Myriad"
   ? "Nuanced"
   ? "Comprehensive" (once is fine, not 5 times)
   ? "Leverage" (use "use", "apply", "employ")
   ? Starting sentences with "Interestingly,"
   ? "In today's digital landscape"

7. BETTER ALTERNATIVES:
   Instead of: "This approach leverages cutting-edge techniques..."
   Write: "This method applies recent advances in..."
   
   Instead of: "The system demonstrates robust performance..."
   Write: "The system performs reliably across..."
   
   Instead of: "We delve into the intricacies of..."
   Write: "We examine the technical details of..."

8. CONNECTING TO YOUR PROJECT:
   For each paper, explain in 1-2 sentences:
   - What problem it solves
   - How it relates to your capstone
   - What limitation you're addressing or extending
   
   Example:
   "While AutoSchemaKG [Chen2025] automates knowledge graph construction,
   it focuses on general-purpose extraction. Our system extends this by
   specializing in brand-specific entities (logos, colors, products) and
   incorporates quality assessment, which AutoSchemaKG does not address."

9. PARAGRAPH STRUCTURE:
   Each paragraph should:
   - Start with a topic sentence (what this group of papers addresses)
   - Discuss 2-3 related papers
   - Compare their approaches
   - Identify gaps or limitations
   - Connect to your project (how you build on or differ from them)

10. LATEX FORMATTING:
    - Use \cite{authorYear} for citations
    - Use \section{}, \subsection{} for structure
    - Include a \begin{table} comparing key approaches (optional but helpful)
    - Use \textit{} for technical terms on first use
    - Use \textbf{} sparingly for emphasis

11. LENGTH GUIDANCE:
    - 2.1 Knowledge Graph Construction: 1.5 pages
    - 2.2 Web Scraping: 1 page
    - 2.3 Image Quality: 0.75 pages
    - 2.4 RAG Systems: 1.5 pages
    - 2.5 Brand Consistency: 1 page
    - 2.6 Feedback Learning: 0.75 pages
    Total: ~6.5 pages

12. CRITICAL: STUDENT PERSPECTIVE
    Acknowledge your position:
    - "Given our constraints (< $15/month budget)..."
    - "For our capstone scope (5-10 users)..."
    - "We prioritize X over Y due to implementation timeline..."
    - "Future work could explore..."

OUTPUT FORMAT:
- Provide complete LaTeX code
- Include \documentclass{article} header with necessary packages
- Include bibliography section with all cited papers
- Use academic two-column format if possible
- Include a comparison table (optional)

BEGIN WITH:
\section{Related Work}

web scraping, image quality assessment, retrieval-augmented generation,
brand consistency enforcement, and preference learning. Our review focuses
on work from 2023-2026, emphasizing production-ready approaches suitable
for resource-constrained environments.

Additional Writing Tips
Natural Transitions (Not AI-Generated)
Instead of repetitive "Moreover", "Furthermore", "Additionally":

"Building on this..."
"A related approach..."
"Similarly,"
"In contrast,"
"This finding suggests..."
"Recent work has shown..."
"Extending this idea..."

Show Understanding, Not Just Summarizing
? Bad: "The paper proposes a method for X."
? Good: "Chen et al. address X by combining Y and Z, achieving W% improvement over baseline B."
Acknowledge Limitations Honestly
? Bad: "This approach is not perfect."
? Good: "While effective for general websites, this method struggles with JavaScript-heavy sites, a challenge we address using Selenium."
Connect Papers to Each Other
? Bad: Review each paper in isolation
? Good: "Unlike GraphRAG [Peng2024], which requires manual schema design, AutoSchemaKG [Chen2025] automates this process, though at the cost of reduced control over entity types."

Sample Opening Paragraph (Reference)
latex\section{Related Work}

Our system integrates several research areas: automated knowledge graph 
construction from web data, image quality assessment, and retrieval-augmented
generation for brand-consistent content. Recent advances in each area have
made our approach feasible within the constraints of a capstone project.
In this section, we examine how prior work addresses these challenges and
identify gaps our system fills.

Final Checklist Before Submission

 No sentence starts with "Delve" or "Leverage"
 "Robust" used ? 2 times total
 Varied transitions (not 10x "Furthermore")
 Every claim cited
 Compared papers to each other, not just listed
 Connected each area to your specific project
 Acknowledged limitations honestly
 Used active voice for your contributions
 Included 1-2 comparison tables
 Length: 6-8 pages
 LaTeX compiles without errors
 Bibliography formatted consistently


Key Principle: Write as if explaining to a technically competent professor who will ask "Why did you choose this approach?" Your literature review should preemptively answer that by showing you understand the field and made informed decisions.
